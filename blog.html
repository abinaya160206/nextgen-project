💔 The Heartbreak at Manchester: India vs New Zealand 💔  
The 2019 ICC Cricket World Cup semifinal between India and New Zealand wasn’t just a match—it was a cinematic thriller stretched across two days, filled with tension, twists, and unforgettable moments.
 Played at Old Trafford, Manchester, this clash saw two cricketing giants battle for a spot in the final, and it ended in one of the most dramatic upsets in World Cup history.
 🏏 Day 1: Rain, Grit, and Kiwi Resolve

      New Zealand won the toss and chose to bat. Their innings was a slow burn—anchored by Kane Williamson (67) and Ross Taylor (74)—as they cautiously built a total of 239/8 in 50 overs.
      The Indian bowlers, especially Bhuvneshwar Kumar (3/43) and Jasprit Bumrah (1/39), kept things tight, never allowing the Kiwis to break free.
      But rain interrupted play, and the match spilled into a reserve day—adding to the suspense.

⚡ Day 2: Collapse and Comeback

     India’s chase began disastrously. In a shocking top-order collapse, India was reduced to 5/3 within the first four overs. 
     Rohit Sharma, Virat Kohli, and KL Rahul—all gone for 1 run each. Matt Henry (3/37) and Trent Boult (2/42) tore through the Indian batting lineup with precision and pace.
     Just when hope seemed lost, Ravindra Jadeja (77 off 59) and MS Dhoni (50 off 72) stitched together a heroic partnership. Jadeja’s counterattack was electric, and Dhoni’s calm presence gave fans a glimmer of belief.
     But then came the moment that broke a billion hearts—Dhoni’s run-out by Martin Guptill’s direct hit. It was the turning point. India was eventually bowled out for 221, falling short by 18 runs.

🌧️ Aftermath: A Nation in Tears

     New Zealand advanced to the final, and India was left to reflect on what could have been.
    The match was a masterclass in pressure cricket—where every run, every misfield, and every decision mattered.
     For Indian fans, it was a bitter pill. But it also showcased the fighting spirit of Jadeja and Dhoni, whose efforts turned a hopeless chase into a near miracle.
